Mongolian Beef with Spring Onions

Description

Delicious beef cooked with a soy-based sauce alongside fresh spring onions. Best served alongside your favorite carbs: rice or noodles!

You can find the original recipe here!

Ingredients

  • 2 tsps. vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp. finely chopped garlic
  • 1/2 tsp. grated fresh ginger root
  • 2/3 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 pound beef flank steak, sliced 1/4 inch thick on the diagonal
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 bunches green onions, cut in 2-inch lengths

Steps

  1. Heat the 2 teaspoons of vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger; cook and stir until fragrant (about 30 seconds).
  2. Stir in brown sugar, soy sauce, and water.
  3. Increase heat to medium-high; stir until sauce boils and slightly thickens (about 4 mintues). Remove sauce from the heat and set aside.
  4. Place beef into a large bowl; add cornstarch and mix until beef is thoroughly coated.
  5. Set aside until most of the cornstarch has been absorbed (about 10 minutes).
  6. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep skillet to 375 F° (190 C°). Shake excess conrstarch from beef slices and drop into hot oil, a few at a time, stirring briefly and frying until edges become crisp (about 2 minutes).
  7. Remove beef with a large slotted spoon; drain on paper towels.
  8. Remove excess oil from the skillet, then heat the skillet over medium heat; add beef slices and stir in prepared sauce. Add green onions and bring to boil; cook until the onions have just softened and are bright green (about 1 to 2 minutes).
  9. Serve and enjoy!
